NBA Timeline

Discover the timeline of the NBA, how it started and how it is going until now.

1946

NBA Begins

The league was founded as the Basketball Association of America (BAA) on June 6, 1946. It began with 11 teams and aimed to bring professional basketball to major arenas in the U.S. and Canada.

1947

First BAA Championship

The Philadelphia Warriors won the first BAA Finals against the Chicago Stags. This championship marked the beginning of professional basketball's rise in popularity.

1949

Formation of the NBA

The BAA merged with the National Basketball League (NBL) to form the National Basketball Association (NBA). The merger unified top teams and players, creating a stronger, more stable league.

The 1950s - Birth of Modern Basketball

1950

Integration of Black Players

Earl Lloyd, Chuck Cooper, and Nat Clifton broke the color barrier by becoming the first Black players in the league. Their arrival marked a historic step toward racial integration in professional sports.

1954

24-Second Shot Clock Introduced

The NBA added the 24-second shot clock to stop teams from stalling. This innovation completely changed the pace of the game and made basketball more exciting.

1956

Bill Russell Sparks the Celtics Dynasty

Drafting Bill Russell created a new era of defense and dominance in Boston. He would lead the Celtics to 11 championships, establishing one of the greatest dynasties in sports.

The 1960s - Celtics Dynasty Era

1960

Lakers Move to Los Angeles

The Minneapolis Lakers relocated to Los Angeles, expanding the NBA to the West Coast. This move created one of the league’s most iconic franchises.

1967

ABA Challenges the NBA

The American Basketball Association was formed and introduced innovations like the 3-point line and a flashier playstyle. The rivalry pushed the NBA to evolve and modernize.

The 1970s - Expansion & Merger

1976

NBA–ABA Merger

The NBA absorbed four ABA teams: Spurs, Nuggets, Pacers, and Nets. The merger also brought the 3-point line and high-flying ABA talent into the league.

1979

Magic Johnson & Larry Bird Enter the League

Magic and Bird revitalized the NBA with their intense rivalry and star power. Their battles laid the foundation for the league’s surge in popularity.

The 1980s - The Golden Era

1980

The 3-Point Line Becomes Official

The NBA officially adopted the 3-point line, adding a new strategic dimension. It would eventually become one of the most important elements of modern basketball.

1984

Michael Jordan Arrives

Michael Jordan was drafted third overall, quickly becoming the future face of the NBA. His impact made basketball a global phenomenon.

The 1990s - Jordan's Dynasty

1992

The Dream Team Dominates the Olympics

The USA sent NBA players to the Olympics for the first time, creating the legendary “Dream Team.” Their performance expanded basketball's popularity worldwide.

1996

NBA 50th Anniversary & Legendary Draft Class

The NBA celebrated its 50th anniversary by honoring the 50 Greatest Players. That same year, future icons like Kobe Bryant, Allen Iverson, and Steve Nash entered the league.

1999

Spurs Capture First Championship

Tim Duncan and David Robinson led the Spurs to their first NBA title. Their disciplined system became the foundation of a long-running dynasty.

The 2000s - New Millennium

2003

LeBron James Enters the NBA

LeBron James was drafted as a generational prospect and immediately lived up to the hype. His career reshaped the league’s modern era.

2004

Malice at the Palace

A massive brawl broke out between players and fans during a Pacers–Pistons game. It led to some of the strictest rule and security reforms in NBA history.

2008

Celtics Big Three Formed

Boston built a superteam with Kevin Garnett, Ray Allen, and Paul Pierce. Their 2008 championship created a new era of superstar team-ups.

The 2010s - Super Team Era

2010

LeBron’s “Decision”

LeBron James announced he would join the Miami Heat, forming a new superteam. The moment reshaped player empowerment and modern free agency.

2015

Warriors Revolutionize the NBA

Stephen Curry and the Warriors captured the championship using a 3-point heavy system. Their style transformed the way basketball is played around the world.

2016

Cavaliers Complete 3–1 Comeback

LeBron James led Cleveland to a historic comeback over the 73-win Warriors. It became one of the most memorable Finals victories ever.

2017

Analytics Era Takes Over the NBA

Teams began using advanced stats to guide strategy, shooting efficiency, and player decisions. This analytics revolution reshaped the league’s style of play.

2019

Raptors Win First Championship

The Toronto Raptors claimed their first-ever NBA title behind Kawhi Leonard’s dominant playoff run. They became the first team outside the U.S. to win the championship.

The 2020s - Global Basketball

2020

NBA Bubble

Due to the global pandemic, the NBA created a controlled “bubble” in Orlando to safely complete the season. The Lakers won the championship under extraordinary conditions.

2022

Warriors Return to Glory

Stephen Curry captured his first Finals MVP as the Warriors won their fourth title in eight years. Their dynasty remained one of the most dominant of the modern era.

2024

Celtics Capture Record 18th Title

Boston reclaimed the crown as the winningest franchise by earning their 18th championship. Led by Jayson Tatum and Jaylen Brown, the Celtics reestablished their dominance.

2025

New Era of Rising Superstars

A new generation—led by Victor Wembanyama, Luka Dončić, Shai Gilgeous-Alexander, and Anthony Edwards—defines the league’s future. The NBA continues to grow worldwide, reaching its highest global audience ever.
